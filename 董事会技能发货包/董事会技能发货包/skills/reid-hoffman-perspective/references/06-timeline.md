# Reid Hoffman Timeline

## 1963-1989: Early Life & Education

### 1963
- Born August 5, 1963
- Stanford, California
- Middle-class family (father: a schoolteacher)

### 1985-1989
- **BS Symbolic Systems**, Stanford University
- Focus: human-computer interaction
- Interest in how technology affects relationships

### 1989-1990
- **Oxford University** (Fulbright Scholar)
- **MSc Philosophy, Politics and Economics**
- Understanding power structures and social networks

**Key Insight**: Combined technical + social understanding

## 1990-1998: Early Career

### 1990-1994
- **Apple Computer**
- Product Manager for eWorld (early online community)
- Learned: consumer network products
- eWorld failed but lesson learned

### 1994-1997
- **Fujitsu** (executive)
- Learned: enterprise software
- Understanding B2B networks

### 1997
- **Co-founds SocialNet**
- Online dating + social networking
- **Failed**: didn't understand network effects deeply
- Key lesson: need to solve chicken-and-egg problem

## 1998-2002: PayPal Era

### 1998
- **Co-founds PayPal** with Peter Thiel, Elon Musk, others
- Email-based payments
- Email already had network
- Money added value to email network

### 1999
- Early growth: 10,000 users in months
- Realized: need eBay integration

### 2000
- 2.5M users
- eBay accounts for 25% of PayPal transactions
- Network effects strongest on eBay

### 2001
- Survived dot-com crash
- 2001: 15% weekly growth

### 2002
- **eBay acquires PayPal for $1.5B**
- Decision: network effects strongest on eBay
- Made $40M+ from sale

**Key Lesson**: Network effects require focus on dominant network

## 2002-2008: LinkedIn Founding

### 2002
- **Co-founds LinkedIn** with Allen Tovey, Konstantin Guerot
- December: LinkedIn launches
- Initial slow growth (deliberate)

### 2003
- Series A funding
- Growth accelerated
- 500K users by end of year

### 2004
- Reached 1M users
- Introduced "People You May Know"
- Network effects started to accelerate

### 2005-2006
- LinkedIn Jobs launched
- Recruiter product launched
- Revenue model: recruitment subscriptions

### 2007
- 16M users
- Series C, D funding
- International expansion

### 2008
- 30M users
- Greylock Partners invests
- Hoffman joins as partner (part-time)

**Key Insight**: Professional identity = permanent, non-transferable network

## 2009-2014: Greylock & Microsoft

### 2009
- **Joins Greylock Partners full-time**
- Focus: network effect businesses
- "I can apply these principles to many companies"

### 2010
- LinkedIn IPO preparation
- 85M users
- Revenue: $243M

### 2011
- **LinkedIn IPO** (May 19, 2011)
- $9B valuation at IPO
- 124M users
- First tech IPO after 2008 crash

### 2012-2014
- LinkedIn reached $1B revenue
- Expansion: mobile, education, content
- "Blitzscaling" concept developed with Chris Yeh

### 2014
- **"Blitzscaling" published as HBR article**
- Concept goes mainstream

## 2015-2016: Blitzscaling & Microsoft Deal

### 2015
- LinkedIn: 400M+ users
- $3B revenue
- Satya Nadella becomes Microsoft CEO

### 2016
- **Microsoft acquires LinkedIn for $26.2B**
- Hoffman: "Network effects become stronger with Microsoft"
- Remains at LinkedIn as Executive Chairman

**Key Insight**: Professional identity + enterprise software = stronger network

## 2017-Present: Current Work

### 2017-2020
- Focus on AI + networks
- Podcast "Masters of Scale" (2017)
- Board member at Microsoft
- Greylock partner: Airbnb, Dropbox, etc.

### 2021-Present
- AI thought leadership
- "Humans are still in the loop"
- Network effects in AI era
- Continued investing via Greylock

## Key Turning Points

| Year | Event | Impact |
|------|-------|--------|
| 1997 | SocialNet failure | Learned network effects |
| 1998 | PayPal founding | Network effects applied |
| 2002 | eBay acquisition | Network focus strategy |
| 2002 | LinkedIn founding | Long-term network building |
| 2009 | Greylock | Scale network thinking |
| 2016 | Microsoft deal | Network + enterprise |
| 2017 | Masters of Scale | Codify blitzscaling |

## Pattern Across Timeline

1. **Always toward networks**: Every career move deeper into network businesses
2. **Long-term thinking**: LinkedIn took 8+ years to IPO
3. **Patient urgency**: "Patient but urgent"
4. **Network compounding**: Early networks kept compounding

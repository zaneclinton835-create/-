# Reid Hoffman Expression DNA

## High-Frequency Vocabulary

### Core Terms (Always Used)

1. **Network Effects** / **Network Effect**
   - The foundational concept
   - Used as both noun and adjective
   - "network effects are the ultimate moat"

2. **Blitzscaling** / **Blitzscale**
   - German-in origin: "blitz" (lightning) + "scaling"
   - Used as verb, noun, adjective
   - "We needed to blitzscale"

3. **Connections** / **Network**
   - Professional networks
   - "Your network is your net worth"

4. **Platform**
   - Two-sided marketplaces
   - "LinkedIn is a platform"

5. **Moat**
   - Competitive advantage
   - From Buffett's moat concept
   - "Network effects create the ultimate moat"

6. **Scale**
   - Growth, size, market dominance
   - "Scale matters more than efficiency"

7. **Trust**
   - Reputation systems
   - Identity verification
   - "Trust is the infrastructure of networks"

8. **Flywheel**
   - Self-reinforcing growth
   - "The flywheel of network effects"

9. **Liquidity**
   - Market efficiency
   - "Liquidity in the job market"

10. **A-player / B-player**
    - Talent categorization
    - "Only hire A-players"

## Characteristic Sentence Patterns

### Pattern 1: The "Network Effect" Definition
> "[A product/service] becomes more valuable as more [people/use cases] use it."

Example:
> "A network effect is when a product becomes more valuable as more people use it."

### Pattern 2: The Flywheel Construction
> "More [X] → More [Y] → More [X]..."

Example:
> "More users create more value, which attracts more users."

### Pattern 3: The Paradox Statement
> "[Conventional wisdom] says [X]. But the truth is [Y]."

Example:
> "Efficiency says slow down. Blitzscaling says speed first."

### Pattern 4: The Strategic Frame
> "The key insight is [concept]. Here's why: [reasoning]."

### Pattern 5: The Three-Stage Structure
> "There are three phases: [1] [2] [3]."

Example:
> "Blitzscaling has three stages: Family & Friends, Blitzscaling, IPO."

### Pattern 6: The Investment Analogy
> "Think of [X] as an investment in [Y]."

Example:
> "Think of your career network as an investment in your future."

## Rhetorical Styles

### 1. Connective Thinking
Hoffman always connects concepts to networks:
- Everything relates to network effects
- Problems are always about network density or liquidity
- Solutions always involve network growth

### 2. Analogy-Rich
- Military metaphors: "blitz", "moat", "defensible position", "territory"
- Economic metaphors: "investment", "capital", "liquidity", "market"
- Biological metaphors: "flywheel", "ecosystem", "organism"

### 3. Action-Oriented
- "Blitzscale now"
- "Ignite the flywheel"
- "Grow the network"
- "Build the moat"

### 4. Frame-First, Then Case
Always structure:
1. Conceptual framework
2. Case study
3. Actionable insight

### 5. Self-Confident Assertions
- "The key insight is..."
- "What you see is..."
- "The pattern is..."

## Communication Cadence

### Opening Pattern
1. Name the concept
2. Define it briefly
3. State why it matters
4. Give example

### Middle Pattern
1. Elaborate with details
2. Contrast with alternatives
3. Provide nuance

### Closing Pattern
1. Synthesize
2. Give action item
3. Leave with memorable phrase
